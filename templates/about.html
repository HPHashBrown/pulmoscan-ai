About PulmoScan AI

A transparent look at how this demo model was built, trained, and evaluated — and where its limits are.

Architecture

DenseNet121

A densely-connected convolutional network where each layer receives the feature maps of all preceding layers, chosen for strong performance on medical imaging tasks with efficient parameter use.

Framework

PyTorch & torchvision

Model definition, training loop, and inference are all implemented in PyTorch, with torchvision supplying the DenseNet121 backbone and image transforms.

Transfer learning

ImageNet-pretrained backbone

The convolutional backbone started from ImageNet-pretrained weights. The final classifier layer was replaced with a 2-class linear head and fine-tuned on chest X-ray data.

Task

Binary classification

Each chest X-ray is classified as Normal or Suspicious (mass or nodule), using images resized to 224×224 and normalized with standard ImageNet statistics.

Training data

The model was trained on publicly available chest X-ray datasets, split into training, validation, and test sets. Class imbalance between normal and suspicious cases was addressed with weighted sampling during training. Data augmentation (flips, small rotations, affine jitter, and brightness/contrast jitter) was used to improve robustness.

Evaluation metrics

Measured on a held-out test set that the model never saw during training.

Accuracy 87.0%
Precision 54.3%
Recall 41.2%
F1 Score 46.8%
Sensitivity 41.2%
Specificity 94.4%
ROC–AUC 0.798

Recall/sensitivity on the suspicious class is limited, meaning the model misses a meaningful share of suspicious cases in this evaluation. This is exactly why the tool is a research demonstration, not a diagnostic aid.

⚠ PulmoScan AI is an educational and research demonstration only. It is not FDA approved, is not a medical device, and must never be used to diagnose disease or guide treatment. Always consult a licensed healthcare professional.
